The Web3 identity landscape is fascinating and fragmented. Here's my
take on the current state:

## ENS as the Foundation

Ethereum Name Service remains the most practical identity layer.
It's simple: your .eth name resolves to your address, your address
resolves to your name.

## Beyond Names

The real opportunity is in attestations and verifiable credentials.
EAS (Ethereum Attestation Service) and similar protocols are building
the infrastructure for on-chain reputation.

## What's Missing

We still don't have a great way to bridge Web2 and Web3 identity.
Sign-in with Ethereum is a start, but the UX gap remains significant
for mainstream adoption.

The stack I'm most excited about: ENS + EAS + Account Abstraction.
This combination could finally make Web3 identity practical for
everyday users.
